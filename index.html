Conor Gillies

Conor is currently Audio Editor at Jacobin, overseeing podcasts and producing the magazine's flagship interview show, Long Reads. He is the creator of People's History, a seasonal podcast exploring social movements and class struggle.

Previously, he worked at WBUR producing Radio Open Source, the first-ever podcast, with a specialty in cultural and music programming. He also developed for WBUR an experiminetal miniseries Stylus, exploring different themes in sound and music. He has written for The Awl and spoken for KCRW about the pitfalls of public radio advertising, which escalated into a minor scuffle with Ira Glass.

Conor was born in Brighton, UK and lives in Providence, Rhode Island with his partner Rosie and their two cats.
