conor gillies

currently, he is an audio producer at radio open source—the first ever podcast—where he researches, records, and edits their weekly show on wbur. he specialises in cultural documentaries, and has recently produced features on the bauhaus, bob dylan, and the art of wildness.

conor is also currently producing an 8 part podcast for jacobin magazine about the history of public housing in boston, and occasionally writes about public radio for magazines like the awl.

conor began in public radio at wbur, producing news for here and now. previously he studied cultural history at boston university, where he won the college prize for excellence in history. his senior thesis on silence, music, and listening was turned into a podcast series and distributed and sponsored by wbur and prx.

born in the uk and raised in maine, conor now lives in somerville with his wife and their two cats. together the four of them run publicradio.info.
